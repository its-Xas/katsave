What We Are

Katsave Sustain Planet International (KASPI) is a nonprofit organization born from a deep reflection on the complex relationship between humans and nature. Its roots trace back to the founder‚Äôs early childhood, shaped by admiration for the melodies of birds, the designs of unique trees, bees, rocks, and hot springs.

This early bond with nature was strengthened through environmental studies and practical work in Uganda, Costa Rica, and at the Wangari Maathai Institute for Peace and Environmental Studies in Nairobi. Exposure to the Earth Charter and Pope Francis‚Äôs encyclical Laudato Si‚Äô further deepened the commitment to ecological stewardship, justice, and spiritual-ecological renewal.

KASPI was founded to tackle urgent challenges such as deforestation, biodiversity loss, climate change, and resource-driven poverty that threaten food security, health, and livelihoods in vulnerable African communities. Through tree planting, community training, research, education, and policy advocacy, KASPI promotes sustainable livelihoods, climate adaptation, and responsible stewardship.

Guided by African indigenous wisdom, the Earth Charter, and Laudato Si‚Äô, KASPI integrates environmental ethics, spirituality, and innovation into practical action for a sustainable, just, and peaceful planet.

African Youth for a Sustainable Planet (AYSP)

KASPI is home to African Youth for a Sustainable Planet (AYSP), a youth-led network that empowers young Africans to become environmental stewards, climate actors, and change-makers.

Inspired by the publication ‚ÄúEarth Charter: A Beacon of African Youth for a Sustainable Planet‚Äù, AYSP highlights the critical role of youth in shaping sustainable development, ethical governance, and climate action.

KASPI also hosts initiatives such as Laudato Si‚Äô, Our Common Future, which serves as a guiding principle for ecological and spiritual renewal in all programs.

Our Philosophy

KASPI believes that humanity and nature are inseparable partners in sustaining life on Earth. Environmental stewardship, social equity, and spiritual-ecological consciousness are fundamental to a thriving and resilient planet.

Guided by African indigenous wisdom, the Earth Charter, and Laudato Si‚Äô, KASPI affirms that sustainable development is achieved not only through innovation and technology, but also through education, ethical leadership, community empowerment, and deep respect for nature.

Every human being‚Äîespecially youth and women‚Äîhas a role to play in preserving ecological balance, promoting justice, and fostering a harmonious coexistence with the Earth. This philosophy inspires transformative action, resilience, and creativity in response to current and future environmental, social, and economic challenges.
